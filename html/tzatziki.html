Tzatziki

This recipe uses mint (which means it's Turkish), but you can substitute dill (to make it Greek). It has quite a bite of raw garlic, so scale back on the number of cloves if you're not into that.

Ingredients

  • 1 cucumber
  • 2 cups plain yogurt
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 bunch mint
  • salt

Instructions

  • Cut the cucumber in half and scoop out the seeds.
  • Grate the cucumber and put it in a strainer. Sprinkle a little salt over it and mix it in. Leave it to drain.
  • Mince the garlic cloves and add them to the yogurt.
  • Chiffonade the mint leaves -- you're aiming for about a 1/4 of a cup. Add them to the yogurt.
  • When the cucumber has drained, squeeze it down to get out as much juice as possible. Mix the cucumber into the rest of the ingredients.

Yield: about 4 cups

Prep time: PT00H10M

Cook time: PT00H20M
